This flautas recipe is origined in Guatemala as an alternative to the Mexican flautas

  • 2 cooked and shredded chicken breasts (you can also use ground beef, mashed potatoes, or refried beans)
  • 1/2 chopped onion
  • 1 garlic clove (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 12 corn tortillas (soft enough to roll without breaking)
  • Sour cream
  • Grated cheese (fresh or aged)
  • Shredded lettuce or cabbage
  • Salsa (red or green)
  • Sliced avocado or guacamole

Steps:

  1. Prepare the filling: Saut√© the onion and garlic in a pan until golden. Add the shredded chicken (or your chosen filling), season with salt and pepper. Cook for a few minutes until everything is well mixed. Set aside.
  2. Warm the tortillas: Heat each tortilla on a skillet until soft and flexible.
  3. Fill and roll: Place filling on each tortilla and roll tightly. Use toothpicks if needed to hold them.
  4. Fry: Heat oil and fry the flautas until golden and crispy. Drain on paper towels.
  5. Serve: Top with your favorite toppings: lettuce, cream, cheese, salsa, and avocado.
