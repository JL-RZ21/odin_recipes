Grilled Steak

  • 2 beef steaks (ribeye, sirloin, or your favorite cut)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil or butter
  • 2 garlic cloves (optional)
  • Fresh rosemary or thyme sprigs (optional)

Preparation Steps

  1. Bring steak to room temperature: Let the steaks sit out for 20–30 minutes before cooking.
  2. Season: Pat the steaks dry and season both sides generously with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat the pan: Preheat a heavy skillet (cast iron is best) over high heat until very hot.
  4. Sear the steak: Add oil or butter. Place the steaks in the pan and sear for 2–4 minutes per side, depending on thickness and preferred doneness.
  5. Add aromatics (optional): During the last minute of cooking, add crushed garlic and rosemary or thyme for extra flavor. Baste with the melted butter.
  6. Rest the steak: Transfer the steaks to a plate and let them rest for 5–10 minutes before slicing. This helps retain juices.
  7. Serve: Slice against the grain and serve with your favorite sides like mashed potatoes, grilled vegetables, or salad.
